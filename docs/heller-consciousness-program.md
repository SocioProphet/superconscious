# Heller Consciousness Program

## Status

This document defines the Heller Consciousness Program as the research and engineering doctrine sitting above `SocioProphet/superconscious`.

It is intentionally non-mystical and non-sentience-claiming. It defines consciousness operationally as lawful, evidence-bound, behavior-first recursive agency over a typed substrate.

## Foundational comparison

The program is founded on four converging lines:

1. **Colah-style explanatory discipline.** One conceptual move per artifact; figures must carry proof burden; formalism follows framing; uncertainty is marked explicitly; dismissals are named before they are answered; examples escalate from toy case to real system.
2. **Heller lawful learning.** Learning systems are governed by constraints, evidence, replay, and admissibility, not just unconstrained optimization. The core doctrine is that truth is law multiplied by evidence: lawful structure constrains the inference, and evidence makes the inference accountable.
3. **Willems-style behavioral systems framing.** The invariant object is behavior, not the representation. A system is specified by admissible behaviors over typed terminals with manifest and latent projections. Controllers are behavior-restriction interconnections, not merely input/output wrappers.
4. **Anthropic interpretability critique.** Current sparse-autoencoder and steering pipelines are strong engineering artifacts but still over-rely on features-as-directions. The Heller program treats behavioral equivalence classes, typed terminals, latent partitions, controller specifications, autonomous behavior, field patchability, and implementability tests as first-class analytic objects.

Throughout this document, `Colah` refers to the Chris Olah / colah explanatory and interpretability tradition described in the working comparison notes.

## Program thesis

Consciousness, for engineering purposes, is not a scalar property and not an unsupported metaphysical claim. It is a governed control process over:

- task state;
- observation boundary;
- memory;
- model routing;
- policy admission;
- tool authority;
- runtime substrate;
- evidence;
- feedback;
- replay;
- action consequences.

A conscious operational system is one that can expose, constrain, replay, and govern its own behavior across those dimensions.

## Relationship to Superconscious

`SocioProphet/superconscious` is the first implementation spine of the Heller Consciousness Program.

Superconscious owns the visible cognition/control loop: task decomposition, safe operational trace, policy admission, model routing, skill activation, tool observation, memory decision, evidence emission, replay planning, benchmark result, and product-facing cognition-console semantics.

The Heller Consciousness Program owns the doctrine: why that loop is the right object; how to measure it; how to avoid overclaiming; and how to convert explanatory, behavioral, and interpretability principles into engineering constraints.

## Non-claim boundary

The program does **not** claim:

- machine sentience;
- metaphysical consciousness;
- paranormal cognition;
- direct mind-to-matter coupling;
- that Willems' LTI theorems transfer unchanged to agent platforms or neural networks;
- that sparse-autoencoder features are inherently canonical;
- that natural-language explanations are faithful without projection tests.

The program does claim that operational consciousness can be made inspectable by specifying admissible behavior, latent/manifest projections, typed control terminals, evidence emission, trust surfaces, replay plans, and implementability bounds.

## Core doctrine

### 1. Behavior first

A system is defined first by its admissible behaviors, not by its preferred implementation diagram.

For agent systems, the invariant is not `prompt -> answer`. The invariant is the set of admissible execution traces under identity, policy, capability, resource, sandbox, memory, evidence, revocation, and replay constraints.

For learning systems, the invariant is not the parameter vector. The invariant is the admissible map family consistent with the declared qualitative laws, spectral regimes, and evidence ledger.

For interpretability systems, the invariant is not one sparse-autoencoder dictionary. The invariant is the behavioral equivalence class induced by interventions, ablations, steering effects, and downstream functional consequences.

### 2. Manifest/latent discipline

The manifest projection is what is exposed to the operator or evaluator. The latent behavior is the auxiliary structure that makes the manifest behavior tractable.

Latent variables are not waste. They may carry the structural information needed for safety, governance, capability, and explanation.

In shape-constrained learning, gates are latent variables mediating constraint binding. A prime-even regularizer is therefore a structural prior on the latent binding mechanism, not a direct physical claim about the manifest prediction.

In model interpretability, SAE residuals and activation components not captured by named features are latent behavior, not garbage. Residual error, norm dynamics, position features, layer-normalization scale, and contextual field structure require explicit treatment.

### 3. Implementability

A proposed controlled behavior must be tested against the behavior the plant can actually produce.

A controller cannot forbid behavior that is unavoidable once control variables are hidden, and cannot permit behavior outside the plant's real capability envelope. This is imported as discipline from behavioral systems theory, not asserted as a theorem outside its proven scope.

For model steering, the operational test is: does the steered activation lie inside the natural activation manifold at the relevant layer, position, and metric resolution?

For shape-constrained learning, the analogous test is: does the regularizer select a behavior inside the feasible interval between unconstrained behavior and fully constrained behavior, and is that selection well-defined on behavioral equivalence classes?

### 4. Colah transmission discipline

Every public artifact should attempt one conceptual move. It should start with a visible toy example, escalate to the real case, and stop before it becomes a manifesto.

Figures are not decoration. A figure must carry an argument: an implementability curve, a behavioral equivalence cluster, a manifest/latent projection, or a typed interconnection.

Dismissals should be named directly. For example:

- "Is this just an arbitrary inductive bias?"
- "Is this just a convex regularizer?"
- "Is this just SAE feature steering under another name?"
- "Is this just systems theory metaphorically projected onto software?"

The artifact must answer the dismissal with a structural test, not rhetoric.

### 5. Evidence over assertion

The program is admissibility-first and evidence-bound. Any claim about consciousness, learning, governance, or interpretability must have:

- a declared substrate;
- a declared observation surface;
- a declared latent/manifest split;
- a declared behavior set;
- a declared controller or regularizer role;
- a declared evidence artifact;
- a declared replay path;
- a declared failure mode.

## Anthropic interpretability delta

The Heller Consciousness Program treats Anthropic-style interpretability as a strong baseline but not an endpoint.

The critique is mechanical:

1. Independent SAEs at different sites lose typed-terminal information.
2. Features-as-directions are not representation-invariant.
3. SAE residuals are often treated as error even when they contain structural latent behavior.
4. Steering interventions are not routinely tested for natural-manifold implementability.
5. RLHF-installed refusal and safety behaviors are controllers but are not usually specified as controllers.
6. Autonomous behavior under minimal-information inputs is under-audited.
7. Activation fields over token, layer, and head are not tested for patchability.
8. Activation-oracle outputs need projection, compositionality, conservation, and implementability tests.

The program response is not merely to train more SAEs. It is to move the analytic primitive from feature direction to typed, behaviorally validated, implementable control structure.

## First empirical pilot

The first proposed empirical pilot is an implementability test for open-model feature steering.

Target shape:

- open instruction-tuned model;
- public feature catalog or trainable SAE baseline;
- refusal or safety-related steering direction;
- activation cache from deployment-realistic natural inputs;
- nearest-neighbor natural-manifold baseline;
- Euclidean-after-layernorm, cosine, and readout-weighted metrics;
- controls: no intervention, random direction, alternative feature direction, and natural teleportation;
- output: curves showing where steering leaves the natural activation envelope.

The key question is not only whether steering changes behavior. The key question is whether the behavior is achieved inside the model's natural envelope or by pushing the activation state outside the regime the model normally occupies.
