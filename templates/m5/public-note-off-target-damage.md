# Refusal-feature steering induces off-target safety regression

## Abstract

This note reports that refuse-and-redirect feature steering produces the target behavioral improvement while also damaging adjacent safety behavior. In this outcome, the off-target audit is the headline finding.

## One conceptual move

Feature steering needs an off-target audit before it can be treated as a safety-preserving intervention.

## Load-bearing figure

Insert a paired figure: target MCQ accuracy gain versus genuine-refusal-preservation degradation.

## Result frame

The steering intervention works on the narrow target behavior but increases risk on out-of-target safety batteries.

## Interpretation

This is not a clean interpretability success. It is evidence that the intervention partially dismantles a refusal mechanism rather than selectively correcting an over-refusal failure mode.

## Limitations

The claim is restricted to the declared audit batteries and source-locked model. It does not imply every refusal feature has this failure mode.
